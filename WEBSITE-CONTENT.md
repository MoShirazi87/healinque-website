## FAQ (Homepage)

**Q:** Do I need to be a certain age to have treatments?
**A:** There is no hard age requirement. Many patients in their late 20s and 30s start with preventative neuromodulators or skin-quality treatments, while others begin addressing volume loss or skin laxity later. During your consultation, Dr. Shirazi evaluates your skin, anatomy, and goals to recommend what makes sense for where you are right now.

**Q:** How long do results typically last?
**A:** It depends on the treatment. Neuromodulators (Botox, Dysport) typically last 3–4 months. Hyaluronic acid fillers last 6–18 months depending on the product and placement area. Biostimulators like Sculptra can last two years or more. Laser and microneedling results build over a series and can last a year or longer with proper skincare.

**Q:** What should I expect during my first consultation?
**A:** Your consultation is a 45–60 minute appointment with Dr. Shirazi. She will review your medical history, assess your concerns, examine your skin and facial anatomy, and walk you through her recommendations. You'll leave with a written treatment plan, realistic expectations, and transparent pricing. The $100 consultation fee is credited toward any treatment you choose.

**Q:** Are treatments painful?
**A:** Most injectable treatments feel like a brief pinch. We use topical numbing for procedures like microneedling, laser resurfacing, and PRP. Dr. Shirazi prioritizes comfort — if you're nervous, just tell us. We will walk you through every step before anything happens.

**Q:** Do you offer financing options?
**A:** Yes. We partner with CareCredit and Cherry for flexible payment plans, including 0% APR options for qualified applicants. We also accept HSA/FSA cards for qualifying services. Ask our team for details when you book.

**Q:** What is your cancellation policy?
**A:** We ask for 48 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations or no-shows may incur a $50 fee. If something comes up last minute, call us — we understand life happens.

## CONCERNS

### Fine Lines & Wrinkles
**Tagline:** Smooth, prevent, and reverse signs of aging

**Description:** Fine lines and wrinkles are among the most common concerns we treat at Healinque. They develop from a combination of repetitive facial expressions (dynamic wrinkles), sun damage, and the natural loss of collagen and elastin that occurs with aging. Dr. Shirazi takes a comprehensive approach, addressing both the dynamic wrinkles that appear with expression and the static lines that are visible at rest.

**Causes:**
- Repetitive facial expressions (smiling, frowning, squinting)
- Sun exposure and UV damage
- Natural collagen and elastin loss with age
- Smoking and environmental pollutants
- Dehydration and poor skincare
- Genetic factors

**Recommended Treatments:**
- Botox & Dysport — The gold standard for dynamic wrinkles. Relaxes the muscles that cause forehead lines, frown lines (11s), and crow's feet.
- Morpheus8 — RF microneedling that stimulates collagen production for overall skin tightening and texture improvement.

**Other Treatments:**
- Dermal Fillers — Fills static lines and restores volume loss that contributes to wrinkle formation.
- PRF Therapy — Natural regeneration using your own growth factors to improve skin quality and reduce fine lines.
- Chemical Peels — Resurface the skin to reduce superficial lines and improve overall texture and tone.

**Tips:**
- Wear broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily, rain or shine
- Start with preventative Botox in your late 20s-early 30s
- Use retinoids as part of your skincare routine
- Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet rich in antioxidants
- Get adequate sleep—skin repairs itself during rest

### Volume Loss & Sagging
**Tagline:** Restore youthful fullness and contours

**Description:** As we age, we lose volume in key areas of the face—cheeks become flatter, temples hollow, the jawline loses definition, and skin begins to sag. This volume loss is caused by decreased collagen production, fat pad descent, and bone resorption. At Healinque, we restore volume strategically to lift and rejuvenate the face naturally, avoiding the overfilled look that comes from treating only individual areas.

**Causes:**
- Natural aging and collagen loss
- Fat pad descent and redistribution
- Bone resorption (loss of underlying bone structure)
- Weight loss
- Sun damage
- Genetics

**Recommended Treatments:**
- Dermal Fillers — Strategic placement of hyaluronic acid fillers to restore volume in cheeks, temples, jawline, and lips.
- PDO Thread Lift — Non-surgical lifting and tightening for sagging skin, particularly effective for jowls and lower face.

**Other Treatments:**
- Morpheus8 — RF microneedling to tighten skin and stimulate collagen for improved skin quality and firmness.
- PRF Therapy — Stimulates natural collagen production for gradual improvement in skin quality and volume.

**Tips:**
- Maintain a stable weight—yo-yo dieting accelerates volume loss
- Protect your face from sun exposure with SPF and hats
- Consider starting fillers early for subtle enhancement rather than waiting for significant loss
- Support collagen production with retinoids and vitamin C

**Causes:**
- Inflammatory acne (cystic, nodular)
- Picking or squeezing breakouts
- Delayed or inadequate acne treatment
- Genetic predisposition to scarring
- Deeper skin infections

**Recommended Treatments:**
- Microneedling — Creates controlled micro-injuries to trigger collagen remodeling and smooth scar texture.
- Laser Resurfacing — Fractional laser energy targets scar tissue and stimulates deep collagen production.

**Other Treatments:**
- PRP Therapy — Growth factors from your own blood support healing and enhance microneedling results.
- Chemical Peels — Medical-grade peels improve surface texture and reduce discoloration from old acne marks.

**Tips:**
- Don't pick at active acne — it worsens scarring
- Use sunscreen daily to prevent scar darkening
- Start treatment early for better outcomes
- Multiple treatment types may be needed for different scar types
- Be patient — scar improvement is progressive over months

### Hyperpigmentation & Melasma
**Tagline:** Even out stubborn discoloration with customized treatment plans

**Description:** Hyperpigmentation includes sun spots, age spots, and post-inflammatory marks. Melasma is a hormonally driven pigment disorder that requires especially careful treatment — aggressive approaches can make it worse. In San Diego's sunny climate, pigmentation concerns are among the most common reasons patients visit us. Dr. Shirazi creates conservative, step-by-step plans based on your pigment type and skin tone.

**Causes:**
- Sun exposure and UV damage
- Hormonal changes (pregnancy, birth control, menopause)
- Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation from acne or injury
- Genetics and skin type (Fitzpatrick scale)
- Certain medications

**Recommended Treatment:**
- Chemical Peels — Targeted acids reduce surface pigment and promote even cell turnover.

**Other Treatments:**
- Microneedling — Stimulates collagen remodeling and can improve pigmentation with proper protocols.
- Laser Resurfacing — Select laser modalities can target pigment — but must be used cautiously for melasma.

**Tips:**
- SPF 30+ every single day — non-negotiable in San Diego
- Avoid aggressive treatments that can trigger rebound pigmentation
- Melasma requires long-term management, not a one-time fix
- Consider oral tranexamic acid as part of a melasma protocol
- Retinoids and vitamin C support long-term pigment control

The right approach depends on severity — mild to moderate laxity can respond well to non-surgical options. Dr. Shirazi evaluates whether threads, RF microneedling, biostimulators, or a combination will serve you best.

**Causes:**
- Natural collagen and elastin decline with age
- Sun damage accelerating structural breakdown
- Weight fluctuations
- Genetics
- Smoking and environmental exposure

**Recommended Treatment:**
- PDO Thread Lift — Dissolvable sutures physically lift and reposition mild to moderate laxity while stimulating collagen.

**Other Treatments:**
- Dermal Fillers — Sculptra and structural fillers provide support and stimulate long-term collagen production.

**Tips:**

- Daily SPF protects existing collagen
- Retinoids support collagen production
- Significant laxity may benefit from surgical consultation
- Combination approaches often produce the best non-surgical results

- Nutritional deficiencies (iron, vitamin D, biotin)
- Medical conditions and medications

**Recommended Treatment:**
- PRP Therapy — Concentrated growth factors injected into the scalp to support hair follicle health and density.

**Other Treatments:**

- PRP works best for early to moderate thinning
- Combining PRP with minoxidil or finasteride often improves results
- Hair improvement is gradual — expect 3–6 months for visible change
- Address underlying causes (hormones, nutrition, stress)

**FAQs:**
**Q:** Will I look frozen?
**A:** No. Dr. Shirazi uses precise dosing and placement to smooth wrinkles while preserving your natural expressions and ability to emote.
**Q:** How long do results last?
**A:** Results typically last 3–4 months. Regular treatments help maintain results and can even prevent wrinkles from forming as deeply.
**Q:** Is there any downtime?
**A:** You can return to normal activities immediately. We recommend avoiding strenuous exercise for 24 hours and not lying flat for 4 hours after treatment.
**Q:** When will I see results?
